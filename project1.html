01 — Overview

Maximising Profit Through Linear Programming

The Pinocchio Problem is a classic linear programming (LP) exercise where a toy company must decide how many trucks and trains to produce each month to maximise profit, subject to constraints on carpentry labour, finishing labour, and available truck demand.

LP is one of the most widely used tools in management science. It translates real business decisions — how much to make, how to allocate resources, which mix maximises return — into a mathematical model that can be solved precisely. Understanding LP means understanding how to find the best answer in a constrained world.

04 — What Did I Learn?

Knowledge, Skills & Reflection

Knowledge

Linear programming transforms a real decision into a solvable mathematical model. The feasible region defined by constraints contains the optimal solution at one of its corner points — never in the interior.

Skills

I practised formulating objective functions, identifying and evaluating corner points, and interpreting the slope of the iso-profit line to determine the optimal production mix.

Reflection

I was surprised that the answer shifts entirely when you change the profit ratio — showing how sensitive decisions are to cost assumptions. LP is a reminder to always check your inputs before trusting the output.
