Project 02 / Network Optimisation

Travelling Salesman Networks

Finding the minimum-cost Hamiltonian tour through a warehouse distribution network

01 — Overview

The Shortest Tour Problem

Network optimisation deals with how to route goods, people, or information most efficiently through a connected system. The Travelling Salesman Problem (TSP) asks: starting from a depot, visit every node exactly once and return — with minimum total distance.

In this exercise, a warehouse (WH) must visit four delivery points — A, B, C, D — and return to base. The challenge is comparing all feasible routes and identifying the minimum-cost tour. This maps directly onto real logistics: courier routing, vehicle scheduling, and supply chain design.

04 — What Did I Learn?

Knowledge, Skills & Reflection

Knowledge

The TSP belongs to a class of NP-hard problems — exact solutions become computationally expensive as network size grows. For small networks, exhaustive enumeration works; for large ones, heuristics are essential.

Skills

I practised reading distance matrices, drawing network graphs, and systematically comparing candidate tours. I learned to spot whether a route is truly minimal or just apparently short due to early cheap edges.

Reflection

It surprised me that the visually "obvious" short route wasn't always the cheapest tour overall. The return leg to WH can make a big difference — something I initially underweighted when scanning routes.
